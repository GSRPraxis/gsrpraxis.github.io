1. WHAT'S ALREADY BUILT INTO index.html
----------------------------------------------------------------

A. META TAGS (in <head>)
   - <title> — rewritten to lead with "IAM, Cybersecurity & AI
     Training for Modern Organizations" (keyword-rich, matches
     search intent rather than just "Trainer")
   - <meta name="description"> — 155-char summary with primary
     keywords (IAM, Cybersecurity, AI, Okta, Entra ID, Oracle IDCS)
   - <meta name="keywords"> — primary + secondary keyword list
     (search engines mostly ignore this now, but some directories
     and AI search tools still read it)
   - Open Graph tags (og:title, og:description, og:image, og:url)
     — controls how the site looks when shared on LinkedIn,
     WhatsApp, Facebook
   - Twitter Card tags — controls preview on X/Twitter
   - geo.region / geo.placename — signals Bengaluru/India location
     for local search relevance
   - canonical URL — prevents duplicate-content issues

B. STRUCTURED DATA (JSON-LD)
   - "Person" schema — tells Google this page represents a real
     person (Ganesh Sathyanarayana Rao), with job title, location,
     LinkedIn profile, and areas of expertise (knowsAbout)
   - "Service" schema — describes the IAM/Cybersecurity/AI/Digital
     Skills training service and area served (India)
   This helps Google show rich results (knowledge panel eligibility,
   better understanding of who you are and what you offer)

C. SEMANTIC HTML STRUCTURE
   - Single <h1> on the page (the hero headline) — contains the
     primary keyword cluster: "Cybersecurity, IAM & AI Training
     for Modern Organizations"
   - <h2> for every major section — each targets a specific
     keyword theme (IAM Training, Cybersecurity Awareness Training,
     AI Training for Employees, Digital Skills Training,
     Experiential Learning)
   - Section IDs (#iam, #cybersecurity, #ai, #digital-skills,
     #experiential, #connect) — used for both navigation AND
     can become anchor-linkable from Google search results

E. IMAGE OPTIMIZATION
   - alt text on logo and portrait images includes name + role
     keywords ("Ganesh Sathyanarayana Rao — IAM Advisor and
     Cybersecurity Consultant")
   - Images are reasonably sized; further compression recommended
     (see Section 4 below)

F. MOBILE OPTIMIZATION
   - Responsive layout: 5 diagonal panels collapse to a vertical
     stack on mobile, sticky nav becomes a slide-out menu
   - viewport meta tag set correctly

G. CRAWLABILITY FILES

     everything, and points to sitemap.xml

     and index them faster

2. WHAT YOU NEED TO DO BEFORE / AFTER GOING LIVE
----------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1 — Choose & connect your domain
   - The meta tags currently reference "https://www.gsrpraxis.com/"
     as a placeholder. Replace this in:
       * index.html (canonical, og:url, og:image, twitter:image,
         JSON-LD "url" and "image" fields)
       * sitemap.xml (all <loc> entries)
       * robots.txt (Sitemap: line)
   - Find-and-replace "gsrpraxis.com" with your actual domain
     once finalized.

STEP 2 — Submit to Google Search Console
   - Go to https://search.google.com/search-console
   - Add your domain (or URL prefix)
   - Verify ownership (DNS TXT record or HTML file method)
   - Submit sitemap.xml under "Sitemaps"
   - Use "URL Inspection" to request indexing of the homepage

STEP 3 — Submit to Bing Webmaster Tools
   - https://www.bing.com/webmasters
   - Bing also powers DuckDuckGo and is increasingly used by AI
     chat tools for citations — worth the 10 minutes

STEP 4 — Set up Google Business Profile
   - Since you're based in Bengaluru and target "IAM Consultant
     India" / "Cybersecurity Trainer India" type searches, a
     Google Business Profile (even as a service-area business
     with no public address) significantly helps local + India-
     wide search visibility
   - https://www.google.com/business/

STEP 5 — Create the Open Graph share image
   - og:image and twitter:image currently point to
     "assets/gsr-social-share.png" — this file does NOT exist yet
   - Create a 1200x630px image (logo + tagline + portrait) and
     save it as assets/gsr-social-share.png
   - This is the image shown when your link is shared on
     LinkedIn, WhatsApp, etc.

3. CONTENT STRATEGY FOR ORGANIC TRAFFIC (ONGOING)
----------------------------------------------------------------

The "Insights" section on the homepage currently lists 6 article
TOPICS as placeholders. These are your highest-leverage SEO asset.

ACTION: Write and publish these as full articles (800-1200 words
each), 2 per month:

   1. Why Identity Is the New Security Perimeter
      Target: "identity security perimeter", "zero trust IAM"

   2. AI Adoption Without AI Awareness Is Risky
      Target: "AI adoption risks", "AI awareness training"

   3. What Every Business Leader Should Know About IAM
      Target: "IAM for business leaders", "what is IAM"

   4. ChatGPT, Copilot and Gemini: A Practical Guide for Employees
      Target: "ChatGPT training for business", "Copilot vs Gemini"

   5. Why Cybersecurity Awareness Programs Fail
      Target: "cybersecurity awareness training", "why security
      training fails"

   6. Building an AI-Ready Workforce
      Target: "AI-ready workforce", "AI literacy programs"

WHERE TO PUBLISH:
   - Ideally on a /insights/ or /blog/ subfolder of your own
     domain (best for your own SEO — every article strengthens
     YOUR site's authority)
   - Cross-post to LinkedIn as articles (drives traffic back
     to your site + builds personal brand)
   - Each article should link back to the relevant service
     section (e.g., the IAM article links to /#iam)

WHY THIS MATTERS MORE THAN SERVICE PAGES:
   Service pages target "I want to buy X" searches (high
   competition). Articles target "I want to understand X"
   searches (lower competition, builds trust, and often
   ranks faster for a new domain).
